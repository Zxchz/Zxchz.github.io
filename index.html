Signal over noise.

I’m a high-school researcher who treats curiosity like an experiment — form a hypothesis, build the rig, measure honestly.

My current focus is audio foundation models: figuring out which neurons actually matter, and pruning the rest without losing the music. Along the way I’ve won a hackathon for a deliberately useless box, written an award-recognized essay on million-qubit quantum machines, and spent a year and a half testing 3D-printing substrates for civil engineering.

I like problems that sit between hardware and theory — where you can measure a result in milliseconds and still argue about what it means.
